# Analysis of Electronic Petitions to Public Authorities

## Project Title and Objective

**Quantitative Analysis of Electronic Petitions**

The objective of this project is to analyze structured open data on electronic petitions in order to identify measurable patterns in public engagement, signature dynamics, and petition success rates.

---

## Open Data Source

## Research Questions / Hypotheses

1. **What is the distribution of collected signatures across petitions?**  
   Hypothesis: The majority of petitions collect a relatively small number of signatures, while only a small percentage reach high support levels (long-tail distribution).

2. **Does the duration of a petition’s availability affect the total number of collected signatures?**  
   Hypothesis: Petitions that remain open for a longer period tend to accumulate more signatures.

3. **What percentage of petitions reach the required signature threshold?**  
   Hypothesis: Only a small fraction of submitted petitions successfully reach the required number of signatures.

4. **Has petition activity changed over time?**  
   Hypothesis: The number of submitted petitions and average signatures per petition vary significantly by year.
